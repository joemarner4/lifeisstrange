Photographer Rob Moorees curated 235 special photos from the Spaarnestad Collection at The Hague’s National Archive. Temporarily released from the archive, these photos became his focus while at Spaarnestad Photo. His aim: to rejuvenate both the photos and their stories. Many photos came from Het Leven Geïllustreerd, a weekly Dutch magazine (1906-1941). I selected 12 images from the book Life is Strange, while focusing on the human body as a main subject.



A portrait of the film star Sylvia Sydney, illustrating an aricle on the attributes of the perfect face. Sylvia Sydney's face was chosen as the ideal of female beauty because of its symmetry and perfectly oval shape. Hollywood 1934.



A man able to pull his skin over his own ears, probably due to burns. He made a living by appearing in theatres and music halls. Berlin 1927.



A woman in basketball clothing, with face and knee protectors. USA 1927.



Unexplained stones. Probably meteors, that fell from the sky. Cincinatti, Ohio 1938.



Woman wearing a mask so as not to be recognized while playing poker for money. London 1932.



This crying baby's parents belong to a Hungarian sect. Half-moons have been carved into the baby's cheeks: a so called 'blood christening'. The baby is a son of Andreas Jankoe, a rich man from Oroshaza. Hungary 1936.



Race research in the Third Reich: a special apparatus is used to determine the position of the ears with respect to facial features in the Kaiser-Wilhelm-Institut. Berlin 1933.



In Britain, a bag of sand was traditionally suspended from the hangman's noose to pre-stretch the rope on the day before the execution. The British legal system was amended in 1939 and many corporal punishments were abolished. Miami 1939.



The 'Divine Eye', the most imporant symbol in Caodaism, a religion established officially in 1926 and practiced in Indochina (todays Vietnam). Caodaism contains elements of Christianity, Buddhism, Confuciarism, Taoism and Islam. My Tho, Vietnam 1938.



Russian partisans hung by the Germans. This photograph is said to have been found in the jacket pocket of a dead German soldier. Soviet Union 1941.



Choosing the Most beautiful back in America: jury member Dr. Coney measures the back of the winner, Florette Dupree. New York 1924.



A man bends an iron bar around the neck of George Challart, The strongest man in London. London 1935.
